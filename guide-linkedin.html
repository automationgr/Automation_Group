💼 LinkedIn Developer App Setup

Step-by-step guide to creating a LinkedIn Developer App and obtaining the Client ID and Client Secret required to enable LinkedIn publishing in Apex Social Automations.

Overview — What You Are Setting Up

This guide is for the platform administrator only Regular users do not need to do any of this. This is a one-time setup done by the platform owner. Once configured, all users can connect their LinkedIn accounts through the simple OAuth flow in the app.

A LinkedIn Developer App allows your platform to publish posts to users' LinkedIn personal profiles and Company Pages on their behalf. The two keys you need are:

# These go in your .env.local file and Vercel Environment Variables
LINKEDIN_CLIENT_ID=your_client_id_here
LINKEDIN_CLIENT_SECRET=your_client_secret_here

1. Prerequisites

💼 What You Need Before Starting

1

A LinkedIn personal account

You must have a LinkedIn account that you are logged in to. This account will be the owner of the developer app. Use your main business LinkedIn account for this.

2

A LinkedIn Company Page (required)

LinkedIn requires that every developer app is associated with a LinkedIn Company Page. This is a LinkedIn requirement — you cannot create a developer app without one. If your business does not yet have a LinkedIn Company Page, you must create one first.

To create a Company Page: log into LinkedIn → click the Work icon (grid of 9 dots) in the top right → click Create a Company Page → choose your page type → fill in your company name, website, and industry → click Create Page.

The Company Page does not need to be fully set up with a logo and description before creating the developer app. You can complete it later.

2. Create the LinkedIn Developer App

💼 Creating the App

1

Go to the LinkedIn Developer Portal

Open your browser and go to https://www.linkedin.com/developers. This is LinkedIn's official developer portal. Make sure you are logged in to your LinkedIn account. Click "My Apps" in the top navigation menu to go to your app dashboard.

2

Click "Create App"

On the My Apps page, click the "Create App" button. A form will appear asking for details about your app.

3

Fill in the App Details form

Complete every field in the form:

  • App Name: Enter a name like Apex Social Automations. This is what users will see when they are asked to authorize the app on LinkedIn.
  • LinkedIn Page: Click the search field and type your LinkedIn Company Page name. Select it from the dropdown. This is the Company Page you created in the prerequisites step.
  • Privacy Policy URL: Enter the URL of your privacy policy page. You can use your website: https://apexrmgroup.com/privacy (create this page if it does not exist yet).
  • App Logo: Upload a square logo image (PNG or JPG, at least 100×100 pixels). You can use your Apex R&M Group logo.
  • Legal Agreement: Check the box to agree to LinkedIn's API Terms of Use.

Once all fields are filled in, click "Create App".

4

App verification by Company Page admin

LinkedIn will send a verification request to your Company Page. You will see a notice saying "Verify this app is associated with [Your Company Page]". You need to complete this verification. Go to your LinkedIn Company Page, and look for an admin notification to verify the app association. Click Verify. This links your developer app to your Company Page officially.

3. Get Your Client ID and Client Secret

💼 Copying the Keys

1

Open the Auth tab of your app

After your app is created, you will be on the app's detail page. Click the "Auth" tab at the top of the app settings. This page shows your authentication credentials.

2

Copy the Client ID

On the Auth page, you will see your Client ID displayed in a field. It looks like a long alphanumeric string such as 78abc1234d....... Click the copy icon next to it or select the text and copy it manually. This is your LINKEDIN_CLIENT_ID. Save it somewhere safe.

Example: LINKEDIN_CLIENT_ID = 78abc1234d......
3

Copy the Client Secret

Just below the Client ID, you will see the Primary Client Secret field. It is hidden by default — click "Show" to reveal it. Copy the secret string. This is your LINKEDIN_CLIENT_SECRET. Store it securely and never share it publicly.

If you ever accidentally expose your Client Secret (for example if you commit it to a public GitHub repository), immediately go back to this page and click "Regenerate" to create a new secret. Update your .env.local and Vercel environment variables with the new value right away.

4. Set Up the OAuth Redirect URL

💼 Redirect URL Configuration

1

Find the Authorized Redirect URLs field

On the same Auth tab of your app, scroll down to find the section called "OAuth 2.0 Settings". Inside it, you will see a field labelled "Authorized Redirect URLs for your app". This is where you tell LinkedIn which URLs are allowed to receive the user's authorization code after they approve access.

2

Add the production callback URL

Click the "Add redirect URL" button. Type in the following URL exactly as written and click Add:

https://Your Domain/api/social/linkedin/callback

After adding it, click Update to save your changes.

For local development, also add: http://localhost:3000/api/social/linkedin/callback

5. Request Required LinkedIn Products

💼 Products & Permissions

1

Go to the Products tab

Click the "Products" tab at the top of your app settings. LinkedIn organizes its API features into "Products" — bundles of related permissions that you request access to. You need to request the specific products that grant posting permissions.

2

Request "Share on LinkedIn"

Find the product called "Share on LinkedIn" in the list. Click "Request Access" next to it. This product grants the w_member_social permission, which allows the app to create, modify, and delete posts on behalf of members (personal profiles). LinkedIn will review your request — approval usually takes a few hours to a few days. You will receive an email notification when approved.

3

Request "Marketing Developer Platform" (for Company Pages)

If you also want to post to LinkedIn Company Pages on behalf of your users, find the product called "Marketing Developer Platform" and click "Request Access". This grants the w_organization_social and r_organization_social permissions. Note that this product may require additional verification by LinkedIn. Fill in any requested information about how you plan to use the API and submit.

The Marketing Developer Platform product requires LinkedIn to manually review and approve your app. This process can take 1–2 weeks. During this time, posting to Company Pages will not work. Personal profile posting (Share on LinkedIn) can be used while waiting for the Company Page product to be approved.
4

Verify the scopes are enabled

Go back to the Auth tab. Scroll down to the "OAuth 2.0 Scopes" section. You should see the following scopes listed as approved:

  • w_member_social — Post, comment, and like on behalf of members
  • w_organization_social — Manage posts on Company Pages
  • r_organization_social — Read Company Page posts and statistics

If any of these are missing, the corresponding product was not yet approved. Wait for the approval email from LinkedIn and check back.

6. Verify Your App is Associated with Your Company Page

💼 App Verification

1

Go to the Settings tab

Click the "Settings" tab in your app. Here you will see the app verification status. LinkedIn requires the app to be verified as belonging to your Company Page before full API access is granted.

2

Complete the verification if not done

If the status shows "Unverified", click the "Verify" button. This will send a verification request to your LinkedIn Company Page admins. Log in to LinkedIn, go to your Company Page, and look for an admin notification asking you to verify the app. Click Approve. Come back to the developer portal — the status should now show "Verified".

7. Add Your Keys to the App

You now have everything you need You have your LINKEDIN_CLIENT_ID and LINKEDIN_CLIENT_SECRET. Add them in two places.

💼 .env.local (Local Development)

1

Update your .env.local file

Open the file .env.local in your project folder and update the LinkedIn lines:

LINKEDIN_CLIENT_ID=78abc1234......
LINKEDIN_CLIENT_SECRET=abcdefghij1234567890abcdefgh

💼 Vercel Environment Variables (Production)

1

Add to Vercel

Log in to vercel.com → open your project → click Settings → Environment Variables. Find or add LINKEDIN_CLIENT_ID and LINKEDIN_CLIENT_SECRET with the values you copied. Select All Environments for each. Click Save. Then redeploy your project for the changes to take effect.
