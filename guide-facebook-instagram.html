Overview — What You Are Setting Up

This guide is for the platform administrator only Regular users of Apex Social Automations do NOT need to do any of this. This setup is done once by the person who owns and runs the platform (the admin). Once these keys are configured in the app's environment variables, all users can connect their Facebook Pages and Instagram accounts using the simple OAuth flow in the app.

Facebook and Instagram use the same developer app. You create one Facebook Developer App, and it handles both Facebook Page publishing and Instagram Professional account publishing through the Graph API. The two keys you need are:

# These go in your .env.local file and Vercel Environment Variables
FACEBOOK_APP_ID=your_app_id_here
FACEBOOK_APP_SECRET=your_app_secret_here

1. Create a Facebook Developer Account

📘 Facebook Developer Registration

1

Go to the Facebook Developer portal

Open your browser and go to https://developers.facebook.com. This is Facebook's official developer portal where all apps are created and managed. You need to be logged in to your Facebook account to proceed.

2

Log in with your Facebook account

Click Log In at the top right of the page. Use the Facebook account that you want to be the developer/owner of this app. This should be your main business Facebook account — the same one that manages your Facebook Page.

3

Register as a developer (first time only)

If this is your first time visiting the developer portal, click "Get Started" at the top of the page. Facebook will ask you to verify your account and accept the Facebook Platform Policy and Developer Terms. Read through them and click Next then Done to complete registration. If you have already registered as a developer before, skip this step — you will go directly to the My Apps dashboard.

Facebook requires your account to have a verified phone number before you can create apps. If prompted, go to your Facebook Settings → Security and Login → Two-Factor Authentication and add your phone number.

2. Create a New Facebook App

📘 Creating the App

1

Open "My Apps" and click "Create App"

After logging in, click My Apps in the top right navigation bar. This takes you to your app dashboard. Click the green "Create App" button in the top right corner of the page.

2

Select the app use case

Facebook will ask what your app does. Select "Other" if you do not see a fitting option, or select "Allow people to log in with their Facebook account (Facebook Login)". Then click Next.

If Facebook asks for an app type, select "Business". This gives access to all the Graph API features needed for posting to Pages and Instagram.
3

Enter your app details

You will see a form asking for:

  • App Name: Enter something descriptive like Apex Social Automations. This name appears to users when they authorize the app with their Facebook account.
  • App Contact Email: Enter your business email address (e.g. info.apexrmgroup@gmail.com). Facebook will send important notifications here.
  • Business Account: If you have a Facebook Business Manager account, link it here. If not, leave it blank for now — you can link it later.

Once filled in, click "Create App". Facebook may ask you to enter your Facebook password as a security confirmation.

4

You are now inside your new app's dashboard

After creation, you will be taken to the App Dashboard. This is the control panel for your Facebook app. You will see a left sidebar with various settings, and the main area showing your App ID prominently at the top. Keep this page open — you will need it in the next steps.

3. Get Your App ID and App Secret

📘 Copying the Keys

1

Find your App ID

On your App Dashboard, look at the top of the page or in the left sidebar. You will see a number labelled App ID — it is a long number like 1234567890123456. This is your FACEBOOK_APP_ID. Copy it and save it somewhere safe.

Example: FACEBOOK_APP_ID = 1234567890123456
2

Find your App Secret

In the left sidebar, click App Settings → Basic. Scroll down to find the App Secret field. It is hidden by default — click the "Show" button next to it. Facebook will ask for your Facebook password to confirm your identity before revealing the secret. Enter your password and click Submit. You will now see a long string of letters and numbers — this is your FACEBOOK_APP_SECRET. Copy it immediately and store it securely.

Never share your App Secret publicly. Do not put it in any public code repository like GitHub. This key gives full control over your Facebook app. Store it only in your .env.local file and Vercel's Environment Variables dashboard.

4. Set Up Facebook Login & Redirect URIs

📘 OAuth Configuration

1

Add the "Facebook Login" product to your app

In the left sidebar of your app dashboard, click "Add Product" (or scroll down to the Products section on the dashboard). Find Facebook Login in the list and click "Set Up" next to it. Choose Web as the platform. On the next screen, it will ask for your website URL — enter https://app.apexrmgroup.com and click Save. Then click Continue until the setup wizard is complete.

2

Configure the OAuth Redirect URIs

After adding Facebook Login, it will appear in your left sidebar. Click Facebook Login → Settings in the sidebar. You will see a field called "Valid OAuth Redirect URIs". This is where you tell Facebook which URLs are allowed to receive the authorization response after a user logs in. Add the following URL exactly as written:

https://app.apexrmgroup.com/api/social/facebook/callback

Click Add after typing the URL, then click Save Changes at the bottom of the page.

If you are also running the app locally for development, add a second redirect URI: http://localhost:3000/api/social/facebook/callback
3

Enable Client OAuth Login and Web OAuth Login

Still on the Facebook Login → Settings page, make sure these two toggles are turned ON:

  • Client OAuth Login: ON
  • Web OAuth Login: ON

Leave everything else at its default setting. Click Save Changes.

5. Add Required Permissions & Scopes

📘 Permissions Configuration

1

Go to App Review → Permissions and Features

In the left sidebar, click App Review → Permissions and Features. This page lists all the permissions your app can request. The permissions listed below are the ones your app requests from users when they connect their Facebook account. You need to ensure these are approved for your app to work in live mode.

2

Required permissions for this app

The following permissions are used by Apex Social Automations. Search for each one and click "Request Advanced Access" for each (this is needed for live/production use):

  • pages_manage_posts — Allows the app to create, edit, and delete posts on Pages the user manages
  • pages_read_engagement — Allows reading Page metrics and engagement data
  • pages_show_list — Allows viewing the list of Pages the user manages
  • instagram_content_publish — Allows publishing photos and videos to Instagram Business accounts
  • instagram_manage_accounts — Allows managing Instagram accounts connected to Pages
  • instagram_manage_insights — Allows reading Instagram insights and metrics
For testing purposes (during development), these permissions work automatically when using the app in Development mode with test users. For live public access, you must submit each permission to Facebook for App Review. This process can take a few days to a few weeks.

6. Enable Instagram in Your Facebook App

📸 Instagram Configuration

1

Add Instagram Graph API product

Go back to your app dashboard. Click "Add Product" in the left sidebar. Find Instagram Graph API in the product list and click "Set Up". This product enables your app to publish content to Instagram Professional accounts via the Graph API. It uses the same App ID and App Secret — no additional keys are required specifically for Instagram.

2

Set the Instagram OAuth Redirect URI

After adding Instagram Graph API, go back to Facebook Login → Settings in the sidebar. In the Valid OAuth Redirect URIs field, also add the Instagram-specific callback URL:

https://app.apexrmgroup.com/api/social/instagram/callback

Click Add and then Save Changes. Both the Facebook and Instagram callback URLs should now be in the list.

3

Add test users for development (optional)

While your app is still in Development mode, only users who are added as "Testers" or "Developers" in the app can authorize it. To add test users, go to App Roles → Roles in the left sidebar. Click Add People under the "Testers" section and enter the Facebook username or email of anyone you want to test with. They will receive a notification to accept the role.

Add your own Facebook account and any other team members who need to test the app. Once the app is in Live mode, any Facebook/Instagram user can connect without being added here.

7. Switch Your App to Live Mode

📘 Going Live

1

Find the app mode toggle

At the top of your App Dashboard, you will see a toggle that currently says Development. In Development mode, only you and your testers can use the app. To allow all users to connect their Facebook accounts, you need to switch to Live mode.

2

Switch to Live mode

Click the toggle to switch from Development to Live. Facebook will warn you about needing a Privacy Policy URL. Enter your Privacy Policy URL — this can be a page on your website. If you do not have one yet, you can create a simple privacy policy page at your website apexrmgroup.com and use that URL. Click Switch Mode to confirm.

Some advanced permissions (like pages_manage_posts) require Facebook App Review before they work in Live mode. If users are seeing permission errors, you may need to submit those permissions for review. Standard permissions like public_profile and email work immediately in Live mode without review.

8. Add Your Keys to the App

You now have everything you need You have your FACEBOOK_APP_ID and FACEBOOK_APP_SECRET. Now add them to your app in two places: your local .env.local file (for development) and Vercel Environment Variables (for production).

📘 Adding to .env.local (Local Development)

1

Open your .env.local file

In your project folder C:\Users\Pascal\Desktop\RUGEMA DCS\Apex\WebSite\Autmation, find and open the file named .env.local in a text editor. Find the lines for FACEBOOK_APP_ID and FACEBOOK_APP_SECRET and replace the placeholder values with the real ones you copied from the Facebook Developer portal.

FACEBOOK_APP_ID=1234567890123456
FACEBOOK_APP_SECRET=abcdef1234567890abcdef1234567890

📘 Adding to Vercel (Production)

1

Go to Vercel Environment Variables

Log in to vercel.com. Go to your project apex-social-automations. Click Settings in the top navigation, then click Environment Variables in the left sidebar.

2

Add or update the variables

Find FACEBOOK_APP_ID and FACEBOOK_APP_SECRET in the list and click Edit to update them with your new values. If they do not exist yet, click Add New, enter the name and value, select All Environments, and click Save. After saving both variables, go to Deployments, click the three-dot menu on the latest deployment, and click Redeploy to apply the new environment variables.
