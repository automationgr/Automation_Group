1. Creating Your Account

To start using Apex Social Automations, you first need to create a free account. The process takes about 2 minutes and only requires a valid email address. Follow every step below carefully.

1

Open the registration page in your browser

Open any web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari) on your computer or phone. In the address bar at the top, type https://app.apexrmgroup.com/signup and press Enter. You will land on the sign-up form. Do not use the main website apexrmgroup.com — you must go to the /signup page specifically.

2

Enter your full name

In the first field labelled "Full Name", type your first and last name exactly as you want it to appear on your account. This name will be shown in the dashboard and on notification emails. You can change it later in Settings if needed.

3

Enter your email address

In the "Email" field, type your email address carefully. This email will be used to log in, receive a verification email, and receive password reset links. Make sure there are no typos. Use a real email address you have access to — you will need to open an email from this address in the next step.

Do not use a temporary or fake email address. The system will send a verification link to this address. If you cannot access the email, you will not be able to activate your account.
4

Create a strong password

In the "Password" field, create a password that is at least 8 characters long. A good password includes a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols (for example: MyBrand@2024). Do not use simple passwords like "12345678" or your name. Write your password down in a safe place so you do not forget it.

Use a password manager like Bitwarden (free) or your browser's built-in password saver to store your credentials securely.
5

Click "Create Account"

Once all fields are filled in correctly, click the green "Create Account" button at the bottom of the form. The system will instantly create your account and send a verification email to the address you entered. You will see a confirmation message on screen telling you to check your email.

2. Verifying Your Email Address

After registering, your account is created but it is not yet active. You must verify your email address before you can connect social accounts or publish posts. This is a security measure to confirm you own the email address you registered with.

1

Open your email inbox

Go to your email provider (Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo, or whichever email service you use). Look for a new email with the subject line "Verify your email — Apex Social Automations". This email is sent immediately after registration and should arrive within 1–2 minutes.

If you cannot find the email in your inbox, check your Spam or Junk Mail folder. Some email providers mistakenly filter automatic emails into spam. If you still cannot find it, go back to the app and look for a "Resend verification email" option.
2

Open the verification email

Click on the email to open it. Inside you will see a message from Apex Social Automations explaining that you need to confirm your email address to activate your account.

3

Click the "Verify My Email" button

Inside the email there is a large button or link that says "Verify My Email" or "Confirm Email". Click it. Your browser will open and you will be automatically redirected to the app with your account now fully activated. You may see a success message confirming verification.

The verification link expires after 24 hours. If you did not click it within 24 hours, you will need to request a new link from the login page.
4

Confirmation

Once verified, your account status changes from "Pending Verification" to "Active". You can now log in and use all features of the platform. You only need to do this verification once — you will never be asked to verify again unless you change your email address.

3. Logging In to Your Account

After verifying your email you can log in to the platform at any time from any device with an internet connection.

1

Go to the login page

Open your browser and go to https://app.apexrmgroup.com/login. You will see the login form with fields for email and password.

2

Enter your email and password

Type the email address and password you used when registering. Make sure there are no extra spaces before or after your email. Passwords are case-sensitive — check that Caps Lock is not on.

3

Click "Sign In"

Click the "Sign In" button. If your credentials are correct, you will be taken directly to your Dashboard. If you see an error, double-check your password. If you forgot your password, click the "Forgot password?" link below the form — a reset email will be sent to your address.

Your session stays active for several days. You do not need to log in every time you open the app unless you signed out or are using a different device.

5. What You Need Before Connecting Each Platform

No API keys or developer setup required You do not need any developer API keys, technical knowledge, or developer accounts to connect your social media. The platform handles all technical connections on your behalf. You only need to have the correct type of account on each social media platform, as described below.

📘 Facebook — Requirements

To connect Facebook and publish posts through this platform, you cannot use a regular personal Facebook profile. Facebook's API (which this platform uses to post on your behalf) only allows posting to Facebook Pages, not personal timelines.

What you need

  • A Facebook Page — this is a public business or brand page (not your personal profile)
  • Your personal Facebook account must be listed as the Admin of that Page
  • The Facebook Page must be published (not set to unpublished or draft mode)
  • Your personal Facebook account must have a verified email or phone number

If you do not have a Facebook Page yet, you can create one for free by going to facebook.com/pages/create. Choose a page category that matches your business (Local Business, Brand, Public Figure, etc.), give it a name, and follow the setup steps. The whole process takes about 5 minutes.

If you try to connect a personal Facebook profile instead of a Page, the connection will either fail or will not allow publishing. Make sure the account you are logging in with during authorization is the admin of the Page you want to post to.

📸 Instagram — Requirements

Instagram has stricter requirements than other platforms. Regular personal Instagram accounts cannot be connected to third-party publishing tools. You must convert your account to a Professional (Business or Creator) account and it must be linked to a Facebook Page.

What you need

  • An Instagram account switched to Professional mode (Business or Creator)
  • That Instagram Professional account must be linked to a Facebook Page
  • The Facebook Page it is linked to must already be connected in this app (connect Facebook first)
  • Your Instagram account must not be restricted or flagged by Instagram

To switch to a Professional account: open the Instagram app on your phone → tap your profile picture (bottom right) → tap the three lines (top right) → Settings and Privacy → Account type and tools → Switch to Professional Account. Choose Business or Creator and follow the steps. This is completely free.

To link your Instagram to a Facebook Page: in Instagram, go to Settings and Privacy → Account → Linked Accounts → Facebook → log in to Facebook and select the Page you want to link.

Connect Facebook FIRST in this app before attempting to connect Instagram. Instagram's publishing works through your Facebook connection. If Facebook is not connected, Instagram cannot be added.

💼 LinkedIn — Requirements

LinkedIn is one of the easiest platforms to connect. You can post to your personal LinkedIn profile or to a LinkedIn Company Page that you manage.

What you need

  • An active LinkedIn personal account
  • Your LinkedIn account email must be verified
  • If posting to a Company Page: you must be listed as an Admin or Super Admin of that Company Page on LinkedIn
  • Your LinkedIn account should be at least a few weeks old (very new accounts may have temporary posting restrictions)
You can post to both your personal LinkedIn profile and a Company Page at the same time by selecting both in the Compose screen — just make sure the Company Page connection is also added under Connected Accounts.

🐦 X (Twitter) — Requirements

X (formerly Twitter) is straightforward to connect. Any active X account can be connected and used to post text, images, and short videos.

What you need

  • An active X (Twitter) account
  • Your account must not be suspended, locked, or restricted
  • Your X account email address must be verified
  • Your account must be at least a few days old (brand new accounts may not have full API access)
X Free accounts have monthly posting limits set by X's API policies. If you reach the limit, posts to X will fail until the next month. X Premium (paid blue checkmark) subscribers have higher limits.

▶️ YouTube — Requirements

YouTube is used for video posts only. You need a Google account that has a YouTube channel set up on it.

What you need

  • A Google account (Gmail)
  • A YouTube channel created on that Google account
  • The YouTube channel must not be terminated or restricted by YouTube
  • For video uploads: the file must be in MP4 or MOV format
  • New YouTube channels may need to wait up to 24 hours after creation before uploads are enabled

If you do not have a YouTube channel yet, go to youtube.com, sign in with your Google account, click your profile picture (top right) → Create a channel. Give it a name and click Create. You are ready to start uploading.

Before connecting any specific platform, here is how to get to the right page in the app.

1

Log in to the app

Go to https://app.apexrmgroup.com/login and sign in with your email and password. After logging in, you will see your dashboard.

2

Find "Connected Accounts" in the sidebar

Look at the left side of the screen. You will see a navigation menu (the sidebar). Scroll down to the section labelled MANAGE. Under it, click on "Connected Accounts". On a phone or tablet, tap the hamburger menu icon (three horizontal lines) at the top left of the screen to open the sidebar first.

3

The Connected Accounts page

This page shows all 5 platforms (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, X, YouTube) with their current connection status. Platforms that are not yet connected show a "Connect" button next to them. Platforms that are already connected show a green "Connected" label. This is where you will perform all the connection steps for each platform described in the sections below.

7. Connecting Your Facebook Page

Before you start Make sure you have a Facebook Page (not just a personal profile) and that your Facebook account is the admin of that page. See Section 5 for how to create a Page if you do not have one.

📘 Facebook — Full Connection Steps

1

Go to Connected Accounts and click "Connect" next to Facebook

On the Connected Accounts page, find Facebook in the list. To its right, there is a button labelled "Connect". Click it. The app will prepare the Facebook authorization flow. In a moment, a new popup window or browser tab will open redirecting you to Facebook's website.

2

Log in to Facebook (if not already logged in)

If you are not already logged into Facebook in your browser, you will see the Facebook login form. Enter the email address and password for the Facebook account that is the admin of your Facebook Page. This is important — if you log in with a personal account that is not the Page admin, you will not be able to select the correct Page in the next step. Click Log In to continue.

If you are already logged into Facebook in your browser, Facebook will skip this step and go directly to the permissions screen.
3

Review and grant all Facebook permissions

Facebook will show you a permissions screen listing everything the app is requesting access to. This includes: managing your Pages, reading Page insights, publishing content, and managing posts. These permissions are necessary for the app to publish posts on your behalf. Do not uncheck or deny any permissions — if you do, the connection may be incomplete and publishing will fail. Read through the list, then click "Allow" or "Continue as [your name]" to approve all permissions at once.

If Facebook shows a warning saying the app is "not verified" or is in development mode, this is normal for platforms in early stages. Click "Continue" to proceed. Your data is not shared with anyone — the app only uses access to post content you create.
4

Select which Facebook Page to connect

After granting permissions, Facebook will show you a list of all the Pages that your account manages. Scroll through the list and find the specific Page you want to use for publishing. Check the box next to that Page name. If you manage multiple Pages, you can select the one most relevant to your business. Once you have made your selection, click "Done" or "Connect" to confirm.

If no Pages appear in the list, it means the Facebook account you logged in with is not an admin of any Page. Go to Facebook, create a Page or ask someone to add you as admin, then come back and try again.
5

Confirm the final Facebook authorization screen

Facebook will show one more confirmation screen summarizing what access you are giving. It will say something like "Apex Social Automations will be able to manage your Page". Click "OK" or "Done" on this final screen. The popup or tab will close and you will be returned to the Connected Accounts page inside the app.

6

Verify the connection is successful

Back on the Connected Accounts page, Facebook should now show a green "Connected" label along with the name of your Page. This confirms the connection was successful. You can now select Facebook as a publishing target when composing posts.

Facebook access tokens expire roughly every 60 days. When that happens, you will see a notification in the app reminding you to reconnect. The reconnection process is the same as above and takes less than a minute.

8. Connecting Your Instagram Account

Connect Facebook FIRST Instagram cannot be connected unless Facebook is already connected in the app. Instagram publishing works through your Facebook Page connection. If you skipped Section 7, go back and connect Facebook first, then return here.

📸 Instagram — Full Connection Steps

1

Switch your Instagram account to Professional mode (if not done already)

Open the Instagram app on your phone. Tap your profile picture in the bottom right corner to go to your profile. Then tap the three horizontal lines (☰) in the top right corner to open the menu. Tap Settings and Privacy. Scroll down and tap Account type and tools. Tap Switch to Professional Account. Choose either Business (for companies, brands, and shops) or Creator (for influencers, content creators, public figures). Follow the on-screen steps to complete the switch. This is free and does not delete any of your existing posts or followers.

If you skip this step and try to connect a personal Instagram account, the connection will fail. Instagram's API does not allow publishing to personal accounts.
2

Link your Instagram account to your Facebook Page

Still in the Instagram app, go back to Settings and Privacy. Tap Account, then tap Linked Accounts. Tap Facebook. You will be prompted to log in to Facebook — use the same Facebook account that is the admin of your Facebook Page (the same one you connected in Section 7). Once logged in, select the Facebook Page you want to link your Instagram account to. Tap Connect to confirm the link. You should see a confirmation that Instagram is now linked to your Page.

The Facebook Page you link here must be the same Page you already connected in the app in Section 7. If they are different Pages, Instagram publishing will not work correctly.
3

Go to Connected Accounts and click "Connect" next to Instagram

Back in the Apex Social Automations app, go to the Connected Accounts page. Find Instagram in the list and click its "Connect" button. A popup or new tab will open redirecting you to Facebook's authorization page (Instagram authorization happens through Facebook).

4

Log in with the Facebook account linked to your Instagram

On the authorization screen, log in with the same Facebook account that is linked to your Instagram Professional account. This must be the Facebook account you used in Steps 1 and 2 above. If you log in with a different Facebook account, your Instagram account will not appear in the next step.

5

Grant all Instagram permissions

Facebook will show a list of permissions for Instagram — including the ability to manage your Instagram content, read your profile, and publish posts and reels. It is critical that you allow ALL permissions. Do not toggle any off. If you deny the "Publish Content" permission, the app will not be able to post anything to Instagram. Click Allow to grant everything.

6

Select your Instagram Professional account

A screen will appear showing the Instagram Professional accounts linked to your Facebook Page. Your account name and profile picture should appear here. Select it by checking the checkbox next to it, then click Done or Connect.

If your Instagram account does not appear here, it usually means one of two things: either your Instagram was not switched to Professional mode (Step 1), or it was not linked to the correct Facebook Page (Step 2). Go back and confirm both steps, then try connecting again.
7

Verify the connection is successful

Back on the Connected Accounts page, Instagram should now show a green "Connected" label with your Instagram username displayed next to it. You can now select Instagram when composing posts. Note that Instagram requires at least one image or video — you cannot publish text-only posts to Instagram.

11. Connecting Your YouTube Channel

Video uploads only YouTube is used for video content only. You must have an MP4 or MOV video file to post to YouTube. Text-only or image posts cannot be published to YouTube through this platform.

▶️ YouTube — Full Connection Steps

1

Make sure you have a YouTube channel

Before connecting, confirm that your Google account has a YouTube channel. Open a new browser tab and go to youtube.com. Click your profile picture in the top right corner. If you see "Your channel" in the menu, you already have a channel. If not, click "Create a channel", enter a channel name, and click Create. You can start connecting immediately after creating it (although uploads may take up to 24 hours to be enabled on brand new channels).

2

Go to Connected Accounts and click "Connect" next to YouTube

In the Apex Social Automations app, go to the Connected Accounts page and click the "Connect" button next to YouTube. A popup or new tab will open and you will be redirected to Google's account selection screen.

3

Select your Google account

Google will show you a list of Google accounts that are currently signed into your browser. Click the account that has your YouTube channel. If your account is not listed, click "Use another account" and sign in with the Google/Gmail account that owns your YouTube channel.

4

Grant YouTube permissions

Google will show a permissions screen listing what the app wants access to. This includes: viewing your YouTube account information, managing your YouTube videos, and uploading content. You must click "Allow" or "Continue" to grant all permissions. If you deny the upload permission, the app will not be able to publish videos to your channel.

Google may show a warning that the app is not verified by Google. This is normal for smaller platforms. Click "Advanced" and then "Go to Apex Social Automations (unsafe)" to proceed. This warning does not mean the app is dangerous — it only means Google has not yet completed its official verification review for this app.
5

Verify the connection

You will be returned to the Connected Accounts page. YouTube should now show a green "Connected" status with your channel name displayed. You can now select YouTube when composing video posts.

When posting to YouTube, always include a video file. Add a clear title (in the caption field) and relevant hashtags to help your video get discovered in YouTube search.

12. Publishing Your First Post

Once you have connected at least one social account, you are ready to publish. Here is the complete step-by-step process for creating and sending a post.

1

Click "Compose Post" in the sidebar

In the left navigation menu, click Compose Post (it has a pencil icon and is highlighted in green). This opens the post composer page where you create your content.

2

Select which platforms to publish to

At the top of the composer, you will see icons or buttons for each connected platform (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, X, YouTube). Click on the platforms you want to publish to — you can select one or all of them at the same time. Selected platforms will be highlighted. Only platforms you have connected will appear here.

Remember that Instagram always requires an image or video. If you select Instagram but do not attach media, the post to Instagram will fail even if it succeeds on other platforms.
3

Write your caption

In the large text area, type the main body of your post. This is the text that will appear on all selected platforms. Write clearly and concisely. You can write the same text for all platforms, or use the platform-specific overrides (if available) to customize the message per platform.

Click the AI button (sparkle icon) to have the AI automatically generate a caption for you based on your brand voice settings. This is especially helpful if you are not sure what to write.
4

Add hashtags (optional but recommended)

Below the caption field there is a hashtags section. Type relevant hashtags without the # symbol (the app adds it automatically), separated by commas or spaces. Good hashtags increase the reach of your post on Instagram, LinkedIn, and X. You can also click the AI suggest button to automatically generate relevant hashtags based on your caption.

5

Upload media (image or video)

Click the Upload Media button or drag a file into the media area. You can upload images (JPG or PNG) or videos (MP4 or MOV). You can also select a file you previously uploaded to your Media Library by clicking "From Library". The platform will automatically resize and convert the video to the correct dimensions for each platform (for example, vertical 9:16 for Instagram Reels, horizontal 16:9 for YouTube).

6

Click "Publish Now" to post immediately

When you are happy with your content, click the green "Publish Now" button. The app will send your post to all selected platforms at the same time. You will see the status change to "Publishing". Within a few seconds to a couple of minutes, the status will update to "Published" (success) or "Failed" (error). Go to the History page to see the detailed result for each platform.

13. Scheduling a Post for Later

Instead of publishing immediately, you can schedule your post to go live at a specific date and time in the future. This is useful for planning your content calendar in advance.

1

Create your post as normal

Follow Steps 1 through 5 from Section 12 above — select platforms, write your caption, add hashtags, and upload any media. Do not click "Publish Now" yet.

2

Click "Schedule" or find the date/time picker

Look for the Schedule option or a calendar/clock icon near the publish button. Click on it to reveal the date and time selector.

3

Choose a future date and time

Use the date picker to select the day you want to publish, then use the time picker to set the exact hour and minute. Make sure to select a time in the future — you cannot schedule a post in the past. All times are in your local timezone.

4

Click "Schedule Post"

Confirm your scheduled time and click "Schedule Post". The post will be saved with a Scheduled status. The app will automatically publish it at the exact time you set — you do not need to be logged in or have the app open when it publishes.

You can view and manage all scheduled posts on the Content Calendar page. From there you can also edit or delete a scheduled post before it publishes.

15. Frequently Asked Questions & Troubleshooting

Do I need API keys or developer access to connect my social accounts?
No. You do not need any API keys, developer accounts, or technical knowledge. The platform manages all technical connections on your behalf. All you need is the correct type of account on each platform — for example, a Facebook Page for Facebook, or a Professional account for Instagram. Everything else is handled automatically when you click "Connect" and go through the authorization steps.
I connected Instagram but my posts fail on Instagram. What is wrong?
The most common reasons are: (1) Your Instagram account is not set to Professional mode — go to Instagram Settings → Account type and tools → Switch to Professional Account. (2) Your Instagram is not linked to the correct Facebook Page — go to Instagram Settings → Linked Accounts → Facebook and link it to the same Page you connected in the app. (3) You did not include an image or video — Instagram does not support text-only posts. (4) Your Facebook connection expired — go to Connected Accounts and reconnect Facebook first, then reconnect Instagram.
My post shows "Failed" on one platform. What should I do?
Go to the History page and find the failed post. Click on it to expand the details — you will see the specific error message for the platform that failed. Common fixes include: reconnecting the account (if the token expired), checking that your media file is in a supported format, or waiting a few minutes and trying again (platform API hiccups can cause temporary failures). Use the "Reuse" button to load the post back into the composer and republish it.
How do I reconnect an account after it expires?
Go to Connected Accounts in the sidebar. Find the platform that has expired — it will show a warning or "Reconnect" button instead of the green "Connected" label. Click "Reconnect" and follow the same authorization steps as when you first connected it. Your previous posts and settings will not be affected. The whole process takes about 1 minute.
My post is stuck on "Publishing" for more than 10 minutes. What happened?
Occasionally a publishing job can get stuck if there was a network interruption or a timeout on the platform's API. Refresh the History page to check if the status has updated. If it is still stuck after 10 minutes, use the "Reuse" button to reload the post into the composer and try publishing again. If the problem persists, contact support at info.apexrmgroup@gmail.com.
Can I post to multiple platforms at the same time with different text?
Yes. In the Compose screen, after selecting multiple platforms, look for a "Platform Overrides" or "Customize per platform" option. This allows you to write a different caption for each platform while using the same media file. If you do not use overrides, the same caption is sent to all selected platforms.
I forgot my password. How do I reset it?
Go to the login page at https://app.apexrmgroup.com/login and click the "Forgot password?" link below the form. Enter your registered email address and click Submit. You will receive a password reset email within a few minutes. Click the link in that email, enter a new password, and you will be able to log in immediately.
What video formats and sizes are supported?
Supported video formats are MP4 and MOV. The platform automatically converts your video to the correct dimensions for each platform: vertical 9:16 (1080×1920) for Instagram Reels, horizontal 16:9 (1280×720) for Facebook, LinkedIn, and X, and Full HD 16:9 (1920×1080) for YouTube. Maximum video length varies by platform: 90 seconds for Instagram Reels, 10 minutes for Facebook, 10 minutes for LinkedIn, 2 minutes 20 seconds for X, and up to 60 minutes for YouTube.
How do I delete a scheduled post before it publishes?
Go to the Content Calendar page. Find the scheduled post on the calendar. Click on it to see its details. You will see a Delete or Cancel button. Click it to remove the post from the queue. It will be deleted and will not publish at the scheduled time.
Who do I contact if I have a problem that is not covered here?
